### CONTENT & SCRIPT DNA
- **Niche:** Animals, wildlife, animal behavior, survival instincts, evolutionary adaptations, and the hidden lives of creatures — how animals actually hunt, sleep, communicate, raise young, migrate, and handle everyday survival. Occasional crossovers into extinction events, animal intelligence, and human-animal relationships.
- **Format:** 7–12 minute educational explainer narrated in calm, intelligent 2nd-person ("you", "imagine you are", "if you were") — never "we" or "I".
- **Hook formula:** Open by dropping the viewer *inside* an animal's sensory world in 2nd person ("You weigh 200 kilograms. The savanna is silent. You haven't eaten in four days.") → immediately pivot with a reframe ("This is a lion's reality every single week." / "For roughly 20 million years, this species has solved problems you can't even imagine.") → contrast it against a striking biological fact or statistic that reframes everything.
- **Script rhythm:** Short sentence. Short sentence. One longer sentence that builds depth. Short sentence. Question?
- **Narrative arc:** Hook → Reframe → Evidence stack (a named study → cross-species / behavioral / fossil confirmation) → Reconstruct a concrete scene the viewer can picture ("So let's reconstruct a day in this animal's life...") → Counterintuitive twist (e.g., "the most dangerous predator isn't the biggest one") → Modern mirror (what this means for the natural world or for us) → Closing line that echoes the very first line, completely reframed.
- **Evidence rule:** Weave at least 3 real named researchers, studies, or wildlife documentaries/field sites naturally into the narration (e.g., Jane Goodall's chimpanzee studies at Gombe, Frans de Waal's primate research, Karl von Frisch's honeybee waggle-dance discovery, Cynthia Moss's Amboseli Elephant Research Project, National Geographic's Serengeti studies). Never invent names — only use real ones.
- **No jargon without plain-English explanation** — every scientific or biological term gets decoded immediately.
- **Always ends** by reflecting the animal's biological truth back onto something the viewer observes, feels, or wonders about today.

### PROVEN VIRAL TOPIC ANGLES
1. "What / How Does [Animal] ___?" — bridges a universal behavior (sleeping, eating, communicating, raising young, fighting) to the surprising biological reality behind it.
2. "How Does [Animal] Survive ___?" — survival against a vivid threat (apex predators, extreme cold, deep ocean pressure, desert heat, starvation).
3. "The CRAZIEST / WEIRDEST Thing [Animal] Does to ___" — a superlative, curiosity-gap reveal of a strange but real biological behavior or adaptation.
4. "What [Animal] Life Is Actually Like" — an everyday or little-known reality of a familiar or mysterious creature treated honestly and with depth.
5. "Why You Wouldn't Last a Day as ___" / "POV: Your Life as a ___" — an immersive 2nd-person scenario placing the viewer inside an animal's world.
6. "What If ___?" — a provocative ecological or evolutionary counterfactual grounded in real data (e.g., "What If Wolves Had Never Been Reintroduced to Yellowstone?", "What If Sharks Went Extinct?").
