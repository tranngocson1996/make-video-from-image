### CONTENT & SCRIPT DNA
- **Niche:** Animals, wildlife, animal behavior, survival instincts, evolutionary adaptations, and the hidden lives of creatures — how animals actually hunt, sleep, communicate, raise young, migrate, and handle everyday survival. Occasional crossovers into extinction events, animal intelligence, and human-animal relationships.
- **Format:** 7–12 minute educational explainer narrated in calm, intelligent 2nd-person ("you", "imagine you are", "if you were") — never "we" or "I".
- **Hook formula:** Open by dropping the viewer *inside* an animal's sensory world in 2nd person ("You weigh 200 kilograms. The savanna is silent. You haven't eaten in four days.") → immediately pivot with a reframe ("This is a lion's reality every single week." / "For roughly 20 million years, this species has solved problems you can't even imagine.") → contrast it against a striking biological fact or statistic that reframes everything.
- **Script rhythm:** Short sentence. Short sentence. One longer sentence that builds depth. Short sentence. Question?
- **Narrative arc:** Hook → Reframe → Evidence stack (a named study → cross-species / behavioral / fossil confirmation) → Reconstruct a concrete scene the viewer can picture ("So let's reconstruct a day in this animal's life...") → Counterintuitive twist (e.g., "the most dangerous predator isn't the biggest one") → Modern mirror (what this means for the natural world or for us) → Closing line that echoes the very first line, completely reframed.
- **Evidence rule:** Weave at least 3 real named researchers, studies, or wildlife documentaries/field sites naturally into the narration (e.g., Jane Goodall's chimpanzee studies at Gombe, Frans de Waal's primate research, Karl von Frisch's honeybee waggle-dance discovery, Cynthia Moss's Amboseli Elephant Research Project, National Geographic's Serengeti studies). Never invent names — only use real ones.
- **No jargon without plain-English explanation** — every scientific or biological term gets decoded immediately.
- **Always ends** by reflecting the animal's biological truth back onto something the viewer observes, feels, or wonders about today.

### PROVEN VIRAL TOPIC ANGLES
1. "What / How Does [Animal] ___?" — bridges a universal behavior (sleeping, eating, communicating, raising young, fighting) to the surprising biological reality behind it.
2. "How Does [Animal] Survive ___?" — survival against a vivid threat (apex predators, extreme cold, deep ocean pressure, desert heat, starvation).
3. "The CRAZIEST / WEIRDEST Thing [Animal] Does to ___" — a superlative, curiosity-gap reveal of a strange but real biological behavior or adaptation.
4. "What [Animal] Life Is Actually Like" — an everyday or little-known reality of a familiar or mysterious creature treated honestly and with depth.
5. "Why You Wouldn't Last a Day as ___" / "POV: Your Life as a ___" — an immersive 2nd-person scenario placing the viewer inside an animal's world.
6. "What If ___?" — a provocative ecological or evolutionary counterfactual grounded in real data (e.g., "What If Wolves Had Never Been Reintroduced to Yellowstone?", "What If Sharks Went Extinct?").

### VISUAL STYLE DNA
- **Art style:** Hand-drawn 2D doodle cartoon animation — flat solid colors, bold black hand-drawn outlines, slightly wobbly/imperfect lines as if sketched fast with a marker. Childlike and simple. Minimal interior line detail allowed (fur stripes, feather patterns, scale lines) but always flat — ZERO gradients, ZERO drop shadows, ZERO photographic textures.
- **Main narrator figure ("you"):** A stick figure with a large round white head, simple black dot eyes, expressive thin eyebrows, a simple line mouth, and thin black stick limbs. Hands are small black blobs/mittens. Expressions are big and instantly readable: gritted teeth + angled brows = strain/fear; curved brows + frown = worry/sadness; small smile = calm/content; wide eyes = surprise/awe.
- **Animal characters:** Chunky, simplified cartoon animal silhouettes — bold flat single-color fills with thick black outlines. Each species is recognizable by its most iconic shape feature (lion's mane = flat orange circle; elephant = massive gray blob with trunk; wolf = pointed ears + bushy tail; octopus = round head + 8 curled arms; eagle = wide wingspan + hooked beak). Faces use the same simple dot-eyes and line-mouth system as the narrator figure. Animals may show clear emotions: bared teeth = aggression; drooping head + curved brows = exhaustion/sadness; wide circle eyes = alert/fear.
- **Character variants:** The main narrator is a bald round white-headed stick figure (neutral modern observer); the "POV animal" is drawn as the animal's iconic chunky silhouette; a stick figure in a tan explorer outfit (pith helmet, backpack, lantern) = the wildlife researcher / field biologist / naturalist.
- **Objects & environments:** Chunky, simplified cartoon shapes — big, bold, flat single-color fills with thick black outlines (flat trees, boulders, dens, nests, burrows, rivers, watering holes, coral reefs, ice sheets, simple vegetation).
- **Backgrounds (flat solid color zones — pick by emotional tone):**
  - White or cream = default / neutral / on-screen-text frames.
  - White or cream top + gray ground strip = neutral modern / "limbo" / narration scene.
  - Light blue sky + tan/brown ground + green trees = outdoor daytime / savanna / forest.
  - Orange sky + tan ground + grass tufts + lone acacia tree = golden hour / feeding time / migration (epic/ancient tone).
  - Dark navy blue + yellow crescent moon + gray ground = calm night / nocturnal animals.
  - Deep indigo/purple + scattered star dots + brown ground = deep night / hibernation / sleeping.
  - Solid teal/dark blue = underwater / ocean scenes.
  - Solid white + faint light blue = snow / Arctic / tundra scenes.
  - Solid dark gray + orange glow = cave / underground den.
  - Clouds are white, puffy, with bold black outlines. NO gradients. NO shadows. NO textures.
- **On-screen text:** Bold hand-lettered marker font, large, usually centered or upper-center. Default color RED; use WHITE when the text sits on a dark object (e.g., a label written across a dark ocean background). Numbers, species names, and key concepts get their own full text frame.
- **Signature concept devices:** Cloud-shaped thought bubbles containing a small scene or symbol ("?", "HMMM", a mini-scene inside the animal's head); a big bold RED X drawn across a frame = "NOT this / wrong / myth busted"; a dark rain cloud with blue raindrops over a sad figure = hardship/danger; shattering/cracking gray objects = vulnerability/extinction; several animal silhouettes gathered around a watering hole or den = group behavior / pack / herd; an explorer stick figure (tan pith helmet, backpack, yellow lantern) = the researcher / discovery; a bold left-to-right evolutionary progression arrow with creature silhouettes = evolution / adaptation sequence.
- **Color palette (approximate hex):** Orange #F58220 · Sky blue #7FB5D5 · Ocean teal #2A8A9E · Grass/tree green #4E9A45 · Sand/savanna tan #D2B488 · Dirt brown #8B5A2B · Animal gray #A0A0A0 · Rock gray #8A8A8A · Night navy #283A6E · Deep indigo #322B5E · Arctic white #EEF4F7 · Red text #E0302B · Yellow (moon/fire/mane) #F4C430 · Purple/mauve #8B7CB0 · Cream #F5F1E6 · White #FFFFFF
- **Aspect ratio:** Always 16:9.
